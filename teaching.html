Current & Recent

Courses

PH 301
Classical Mechanics
Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of mechanics, canonical transformations, Hamilton–Jacobi theory, and introduction to chaos. Core graduate course for all physics students.
Graduate3 creditsAutumn semester
PH 401
Quantum Mechanics
Postulates of quantum mechanics, Dirac notation, angular momentum, perturbation theory, scattering, and identical particles. Essential foundation for all theoretical and computational work in the group.
Graduate3 creditsSpring semester
PH 502
Statistical Mechanics
Microcanonical, canonical, and grand-canonical ensembles; thermodynamics from statistical foundations; ideal and non-ideal gases; phase transitions; introduction to Monte Carlo and MD methods.
Graduate3 creditsAutumn semester
PH 605
Computational Physics
Numerical methods for physics: ODE/PDE solvers, Monte Carlo, molecular dynamics, density functional theory, and machine-learning methods. Students complete a research-level computational project.
Graduate3 creditsSpring semester
PH 201
Electricity & Magnetism
Electrostatics, magnetostatics, Faraday's law, Maxwell's equations, electromagnetic waves, and radiation. Core undergraduate course with an emphasis on problem-solving and physical intuition.
Undergraduate3 creditsAutumn semester
PH 650
Multiscale Methods in Materials Science
Advanced elective covering density functional theory, molecular dynamics, coarse-grained methods, and machine-learning potentials — the core tools of the SCALE group. Open to Physics, Chemistry, and Materials Science students.
Graduate Elective3 creditsSpring semester
For Students

Student Resources

📚

Lecture Materials

Course slides, problem sets, and supplementary readings are distributed via IIT Bhubaneswar's learning management system. Enrolled students receive access at the start of each semester.

🖥

Computational Resources

Students in PH 605 and PH 650 have access to the group's HPC cluster and to national supercomputing facilities through IIT Bhubaneswar's allocations on PARAM and CDAC systems.

🎓

Final Year Projects

We regularly supervise BTech and MSc final-year projects across all four research areas. Interested students should contact Dr. Kumar at least one semester in advance with a brief statement of interest.

🌐

Office Hours

Dr. Kumar holds weekly office hours for enrolled students. Research group meetings are held every Friday. Prospective students are welcome to attend the group's open seminars — contact us to be added to the mailing list.
