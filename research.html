Research

Our research focuses on structure-property relationships across length and energy scales, from nanoscale materials and confined liquids to soft-matter and biological systems.

We use multiscale modelling rooted in soft matter physics, statistical mechanics, and atomistic simulation to study ionic transport, composite electrolytes, energy-material interfaces, and polymer models of genome organization.

Across these systems, molecular dynamics, coarse-grained models, machine-learning potentials, and kinetic Monte Carlo methods help connect microscopic structure and dynamics to mechanisms, predictions, and design rules.
